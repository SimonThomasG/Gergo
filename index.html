This program was designed to assist individuals dealing with lower-back issues or aiming to prevent them. Given the prevalent occurrence of lower-back pain, particularly due to prolonged periods of sitting, this program is intended to address this common concern. The program I provide consists of a thoughtfully curated series of exercises that both alleviate and prevent lower-back pain. It encompasses mobilization, SMR, and stretching exercises. While each exercise has a positive impact on the body independently, their combined use enhances their effectiveness. These exercises are suitable for individuals of all ages and physical conditions, with the exception of those dealing with severe spinal problems or hernias. The exercises are structured to cater to different proficiency levels, ranging from non-athletes to athletes.

Methods That Work

Mobility Training

Mobility training involves restoring and preventing the restriction of joint motion. By engaging in simple, load-free exercises that are accessible to everyone, we guide the joints through their entire range of motion. The enhanced movement of the joints positively impacts the surrounding muscles and joints, facilitating the execution of subsequent exercises.

Utilizing a Self Myofascial Release Roller or Foam Roller has a positive impact on the myofascial system. This practice increases the body's overall range of motion and provides relief from pain. It effectively relaxes tight muscles, releasing tension. Professionally guided exercises during the sessions ensure that you learn the correct techniques for optimal results.

Stretching

Stretching plays a crucial role in maintaining muscle health and preventing injuries. Flexible muscles contribute to easier movement, regardless of the type of activity. Similar to mobility excercieses and foam rolling, stretching contributes to expanding the body's range of motion. During the sessions, we focus on static stretching, targeting areas that alleviate tension around the lower back.

It all commenced with an injury many years ago. I took a hiatus from practicing martial arts for several months, and upon resuming, it felt as if there had been no interruption. My body seemed to operate differently! Amidst a strenuous workout, a misstep resulted in a sharp pain in my lower back. Taking it as a signal to slow down and gradually return to optimal shape, I acknowledged that, from that point onward, I was injured and unable to engage in even a light workout. In pursuit of a solution, I sought ways to address my back issue and return to serious training. I experimented with various traditional and alternative treatments for the lower back, but the only one that genuinely helped was a chiropractic session. Despite personal skepticism, I gave it a shot with the intent to heal. Surprisingly, it worked! Post-session, my lower back felt significantly improved, and albeit gradually, I resumed my workout routine. This experience heightened my curiosity about the human body and its mechanisms. How could one effective technique make such a difference and enable me to surpass previous limitations? I needed answers! Over the years, I delved deeper into studies, accessing materials from the University of Physical Education in Budapest, undergoing chiropractic courses, studying the functions and techniques of SMR, and becoming acquainted with the Ming method developed by Ming Chew. Practical application and self-treatment, alongside theoretical knowledge, contributed to the enrichment of my studies. The amalgamation of learnings and practical experience empowered me to devise a program that effectively addressed my problem. As an active sportsman and martial artist, this program became more of a preventive measure for me. After some time, friends experiencing similar issues approached me, seeking advice on alleviating such pain. Eager to assist, I streamlined the program to offer something accessible yet effective to everyone. Those who tried it out expressed satisfaction; the program proved successful. This marked a turning point. I realized that if I could assist my friends, why not extend help to others also grappling with lower-back pain or fearing its potential onset? This is precisely why I am here: to aid in the treatment and prevention of lower-back pain!
